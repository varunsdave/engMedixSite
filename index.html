Our Story

Founded in 2014, ENGMedix is a startup company based out of Waterloo, Ontario. Our goal is to create a software platform with the utilization of fitness wearables that allow injured/rehabilitation patients to monitor their exercise routines and track their progress while getting real-time feedback from a wearable device. This allows the patient to engage themselves into their recovery process, resulting in higher motivation and improved diagnosis and treatment by their physiotherapist. We are currently using the MYO armband, which is a gesture control device and in conjunction with our software application, rehab patients will now be able to do their exercise routines correctly in the comfort of their own home. The application monitors the user's range of motion during their exercise routines and instantly makes the data available to share with their physiotherapist online. This allows the physiotherapists to view the the patients' exercise data over a certain period of time while tracking their improvement. Our next goal is to apply for a space in the Velocity Incubator in order to help improve our product.

Our Consultants

Rob Burns Head Athletic Therapist

For the past 17 years Rob has been practicing athletic therapy and physical education focusing in Canadian University Sport. In the last 10 years Rob has been working under the University of Waterloo in the faculty of Applied Health Sciences in the Kinesiology department. Rob coordinates and lectures a number of sport injury courses. His teaching philosophy consists of learning by doing, therefore what is taught in the classroom is translated into real world practical situations, he currently trains student trainers and Sheridan College Athletic Therapy placement students. Rob also serves as the Head Athletic Therapist at the University, coordinating and managing a variety of health care providers that form the athletic therapy team at the University of Waterloo.

Shi Cao Assistant Professor

Dr. Cao studies human factors and ergonomics using both experiment and computational simulation methods. His current research focuses on modeling human performance and mental workload in complex human-machine interactions and developing cognitive engineering tools and innovative interfaces for quantitative human factors analysis and human performance optimization.

He has ongoing research in the areas of

  1. Human factors and cognitive ergonomics (driving, healthcare, aerospace).
  2. Human performance and mental workload modelling, cognitive modelling.
  3. Human-computer interaction, interface evaluation and design.
